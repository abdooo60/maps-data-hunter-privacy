Privacy Policy for Maps Data Hunter

Last updated: 24 December 2025

1. Introduction

Maps Data Hunter (“we”, “our”, or “the extension”) is a Chrome extension designed to help users extract publicly available business information from Google Maps for research and productivity purposes.

We respect your privacy and are committed to being transparent about how data is handled.

2. Information We Collect

Maps Data Hunter collects limited, non-personal data necessary for the extension to function properly.

We may collect:

  • Usage Data: The number of businesses extracted, used to enforce free usage limits.
  • Anonymous Device Identifier: A randomly generated, non-identifiable identifier used to track usage across sessions and prevent abuse.
  • License Information (Premium users only): License keys are stored and validated to enable premium features.

3. Information We Do NOT Collect

We do not collect:

  • Names, email addresses, or phone numbers of users
  • Google account information
  • Browsing history outside Google Maps
  • Payment or financial information
  • The business data you extract from Google Maps

4. How We Use Information

Collected information is used solely to:

  • Track usage and enforce free-tier limits
  • Validate premium licenses
  • Prevent misuse of the extension
  • Maintain and improve extension functionality

5. Data Storage and Sharing

  • Usage and license-related data may be securely stored on our servers.
  • No extracted business data is transmitted or stored externally.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share any user data with third parties.

6. Permissions

Maps Data Hunter requests only the minimum permissions required to:

  • Access Google Maps pages
  • Extract publicly available business information

These permissions are used strictly for the extension’s core functionality.

7. Third-Party Services

This extension does not use third-party analytics, advertising networks, or tracking services.

8. Children’s Privacy

Maps Data Hunter is not intended for use by children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated revision date.
